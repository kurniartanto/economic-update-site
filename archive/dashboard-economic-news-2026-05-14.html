Executive Summary

Briefing points for market monitoring, sovereign debt strategy, and Indonesia spillover assessment.

  • U.S. inflation shock dominated the window. April PPI rose well above consensus, shifting the near-term narrative toward higher UST yields, stronger USD, and tighter global financial conditions.
  • Oil remains the second major shock. IEA and OPEC updates point to supply disruption, inventory drawdowns, and elevated fuel-price risk from the Iran/Hormuz channel.
  • Trump–Xi diplomacy offered partial risk support, but not a clean risk-on signal. Trade talks may stabilize sentiment, yet Taiwan warnings and China–EU investment friction keep geopolitical risk alive.
  • Global equities were resilient, but this is not automatically supportive for EM bonds. A combination of rising equities, firmer dollar, and sticky yields can still pressure rupiah and SBN duration.
  • Japan FX intervention risk matters for Asia. USD/JPY around intervention-sensitive levels can spill over to Asian FX psychology and worsen IDR volatility if DXY strengthens.
  • Indonesia implication is cautious rather than outright adverse. Watch UST, USD, oil, CNY/JPY, and non-resident SBN flows; APBN energy sensitivity deserves close monitoring.

Why This Matters for Indonesia and Investors

Transmission from global macro shocks into Indonesia’s financing, bonds, FX, equities, APBN, and sovereign risk premium.

Indonesia relevance panel

UST yield and global bond yield: Hot U.S. PPI reduces room for Fed easing and can push term premium higher. SBN pricing can be pressured through benchmark-rate and global-duration channels.
Yield SBN and IHSG: SBN tenor menengah-panjang is vulnerable to UST repricing. IHSG can receive support from global equity resilience, but rate-sensitive and import-cost sectors remain exposed.
Rupiah: Stronger USD, USD/JPY volatility, and China headline risk can pressure Asian FX. CNY and JPY should be monitored as regional anchors.
Capital flow: EM local-currency bond demand may become more selective if global yields and USD remain firm. Foreign participation in SBN requires close monitoring.
APBN and debt financing: Oil shock raises subsidy/compensation sensitivity and can complicate financing cost assumptions if market yields rise simultaneously.
Risk appetite toward EM assets: The mix of resilient equity and high rates is not fully benign. It can favor global tech while still penalizing EM duration and FX.
Commodity prices: Oil shock is negative for energy-import and inflation channels; selected commodity revenue channels may help, but net APBN effect depends on price formula and policy response.
Monitoring strategy: Prioritize UST 10Y/30Y, DXY, Brent/WTI, USD/JPY, CNY fixing, CDS, non-resident SBN flows, and SUN auction bid-to-cover/pricing.
